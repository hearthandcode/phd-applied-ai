# Session Log — 2026-06-13

**Session type:** Meta-reflection (non-study)
**Summary:** Launch day for Substack Post 3 and the phd-applied-ai public GitHub repo.
First external comment received. Significant anxiety presentation around public exposure,
privacy, license integrity, and epistemic confidence. Engaged AI assistant for structured
review of concerns and continued engagement with the project despite distress.

## Context and Setting

Today was the first day of public engagement for the curriculum project. Substack Post 3
("I Gave Myself a PhD. Here's What That Actually Looks Like.") was published. The
phd-applied-ai GitHub repository, Phase A scaffold complete, was live. Social posts
went out on X (Twitter) and Reddit (r/learnmachinelearning). Shortly after posting,
the first external comment was received on Reddit: a request for clearer usage
instructions and a video tutorial.

No module study occurred. The session consisted of public-facing activity, processing
the first external feedback, and a significant period of anxiety processing that was
brought to the AI assistant for structured review and support.

## Psychological State (Clinical Description)

Self-reported psychological state during this session was one of significant anxiety,
coexisting with continued motivated engagement with the project. The anxiety presentation
was multidimensional — not a single concern but a cluster of distinct but interrelated
stressors (detailed below).

The subject demonstrated clear meta-cognitive awareness of the anxiety state, naming it
directly ("I've been wrecking my head on it"), identifying it as distinct from his
underlying motivation, and actively seeking to evaluate whether specific concerns
represented valid risk or anxiety-amplified perception of manageable situations.

This meta-cognitive monitoring capacity under stress is consistent with the protective
factor observed in individuals who have developed explicit coping frameworks for ADHD
and bipolar presentations. The ability to simultaneously experience significant anxiety
and evaluate it analytically is a notable cognitive signature.

Mood: anxious-motivated. The anxiety did not displace the motivation; both were present
concurrently. Subject continued to take productive action (drafting Reddit response,
adding documentation, logging the session) throughout the distress period.

---

## Primary Stressors (Self-Report)

The following concerns were self-reported and presented as the content of the anxiety:

**1. Privacy and personal security**
Concerns about personal information exposure: full name, mental health diagnosis (ADHD
and bipolar disorder), and public web presence (hearthandcode.dev). Specific worries
included doxxing, hacking, identity theft, and unauthorized use of personal data.

**2. License and contribution structure perception**
Concern that the CC BY-NC-SA 4.0 license with CLA (contributor license agreement
granting commercial use rights to the copyright holder) would be perceived as exploitative
or deceptive. Subject framed the concern as: "Are people going to think I'm wrong by
using that license and trying to keep contributions for commercial purposes so I can
profit and use it."

**3. Content quality and AI-generated material perception**
Concern that the curriculum's AI-assisted content generation would lead to dismissal
as "AI slop" — a recognized pejorative for low-quality, unreviewed AI-generated content.
Secondarily, concern about the subject's own epistemic reliability in evaluating
AI-generated content given past experience: "I let AI generate a lot of content for me
thinking it was factual but it wasn't."

**4. Epistemic confidence calibration**
Subject raised a self-reflective concern about whether his confidence in the quality of
the work is "misplaced confidence in AI generative models and not good sound human judgment."
This represents a healthy epistemic posture — genuine uncertainty about one's own
evaluative accuracy rather than either uncritical acceptance or blanket rejection of
AI-assisted work.

**5. First external feedback received**
The Reddit comment requesting usage instructions was the first external signal that the
project had reached a real audience. This is a significant psychological threshold in
"building in public" — the transition from internal project to externally engaged project.
The concurrent presence of this milestone and the anxiety state is clinically notable.
