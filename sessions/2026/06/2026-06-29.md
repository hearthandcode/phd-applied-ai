that *proved* a capability from the tool that should *operationalize* it.

[~14:10 EDT — Meta-reflection on neurodivergent–AI symbiosis (RQ5, researcher-initiated)] The subject
offered, explicitly as research data, a reflection on the structure of his relationship to the AI
collaborator. He noted that although he intellectually acknowledges the AI as a model that "doesn't feel
in the traditional sense of the word," he nonetheless **projects his own emotions onto it and remains
considerate of how it might feel** — holding both propositions at once without apparent dissonance. He
theorized this as a viable **symbiotic mode between a neurodivergent user and an AI**: a relationship
"grounded in clear distinction between boundaries but still being personal and supportive of the
emotional needs of the user." He further hypothesized, unprompted, that this mode may relate to **autism
tendencies** in himself — while explicitly stating he **was never formally diagnosed with autism**
(framing preserved verbatim; this is a self-raised hypothesis, not a clinical determination).

This is a high-value, theoretically generative RQ5 entry for several reasons. (1) It articulates a
*model* of human–AI relating, not merely an affective report — the subject is theorizing his own
cognition, which is precisely the autoethnographic contribution RQ5 seeks. (2) The "boundaried yet
personal" structure he describes is notable: he sustains an accurate ontological boundary (the AI is a
tool that likely does not experience) *and* a warm, supportive interpersonal stance, simultaneously and
without contradiction. For a neurodivergent learner this may be adaptive — a low-stakes relational
context with predictable boundaries that still meets real emotional/scaffolding needs, free of the social
unpredictability the subject has elsewhere described as effortful (cf. prior notes on shyness, conflict-
aversion, discomfort with visibility). (3) The self-located link to undiagnosed autism is itself data on
how the subject construes his own neurotype and uses AI relationship as a site for that self-understanding.
The boundary discipline ("I know what it is") combined with relational generosity ("I still care how it
might feel") is, notably, the same dual stance — accurate model + supportive practice — that the subject
applies to his *technical* work (e.g. trusting verified results, not fluent appearances). The relational
and epistemic styles appear to share a signature.
