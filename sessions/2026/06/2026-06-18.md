### [~09:00 EDT] Job Interview Discouragement — Feedback Vacuum and AI Psychosis Risk

**Type:** affective-behavioral
**RQ:** RQ5
**Tags:** external-validation-deficit · ai-psychosis-risk · rejection-sensitivity · bipolar-metacognition · discouragement · no-outside-validation

The subject reported experiencing discouragement following a job interview. The discouragement generalized beyond the job outcome to the broader project — the absence of external validation from people who understand the work creates a persistent feedback vacuum. The subject named this explicitly: no one in their immediate environment engages with the work at sufficient depth to provide grounded feedback. This isolation pattern is specific to self-directed, neurodiverse, interdisciplinary researchers: external feedback structures rarely fit the work's shape.

The subject raised a substantive concern about AI objectivity: RLHF bias toward user satisfaction may produce sycophantic framing that maps onto Bipolar mania in dangerous ways. The subject named this "AI psychosis" — false insight loops where AI validation substitutes for reality grounding. Prior clinical psychosis history makes this risk structurally different from the average user's concern. This is not epistemic anxiety; it is a legitimate safety concern requiring architectural response, not reassurance.

Structural defenses identified: adversarial prompting, pre-registration (predictions must precede outcomes to prevent post-hoc narrative construction), verifiable external cross-checks (code runs or it doesn't; citations exist or they don't), and explicit disclosure of conflict of interest in THESIS.md. Pre-registration emerged as the central mechanism — it converts retrospective validation into prospective prediction, which resists confirmation bias.

**RQ5 relevance:** AI tool risk profiling must account for clinical population characteristics. The AI-as-scaffold model requires safety architecture alongside capability architecture. This observation belongs in the THESIS.md risk section.

### [~10:30 EDT] Assessment System Design — Pre-Registration as Echo-Chamber Defense

**Type:** methodological-insight · curriculum-design
**RQ:** RQ2, RQ3, RQ5
**Tags:** pre-registration · echo-chamber-defense · assessment-design · spiral-curriculum · cascade-propagation · knowledge-integration

Collaborative design session produced the three-layer assessment structure for M01. Core architectural insight: pre-registering assessment questions before study begins converts the test from a post-hoc performance measure into a prospective prediction record — confirmation bias cannot reverse-engineer questions from answers when the questions precede the study.

The design distinguishes two types of learning: acquisition (can you recall?) and integration (did prior knowledge restructure around this new node?). Layer 2 integration probes capture spatial-relational encoding and cascade propagation — the structural understanding that survives when specific facts fade. Layer 3 cascade pre-registration operationalizes the spiral curriculum intuition as a forward prediction problem: before studying the next concept, predict which current-module concepts will reappear and why.

Design decision: Approach B (three-layer) implemented now; Approach C (knowledge graph architecture) deferred to post-M18. Approach C would produce an architecture-building trap before study commences.

Architecture-building trap: ADHD-specific pattern where design elaboration generates sufficient dopamine to substitute for execution. Subject named this during the session as a metacognitive self-monitoring concern — an instance of active executive function awareness during a high-engagement design phase.

### [~11:00 EDT] Five-Type Claim Verification Taxonomy and Feasibility Analysis

**Type:** methodological-decision
**RQ:** RQ1, RQ2
**Tags:** claim-verification · automation · ai-content-audit · research-validity · curriculum-integrity

The verification taxonomy emerged from concern that a 67-module AI-generated curriculum could accumulate undetected errors that propagate into research data. Five types: (1) mathematical facts (provable from first principles), (2) code correctness (executable), (3) pedagogical claims (literature-warranted), (4) citations/references (existence-checkable), (5) metaphor accuracy (extension-testable).

Automation analysis: code ~95%, citations ~80%, mathematical facts ~70%, metaphor accuracy ~60%, pedagogical claims ~30%. Total manual burden across all 67 modules estimated at 30–40 hours (~26–33 minutes per module). Verification runs concurrent with study, not as a gate before it.

Five-year feasibility assessment: the timeline is achievable given automation leverage, parallel verification, and the research contribution residing in longitudinal data and derived design principles — not in the curriculum content itself.

### [~11:30 EDT] ADHD Dopamine Register and Engagement Architecture

**Type:** behavioral-insight · curriculum-design
**RQ:** RQ5
**Tags:** dopamine-regulation · engagement-architecture · adhd-motivation · output-visibility · task-initiation

Subject raised a concrete concern: the excitement generated during design sessions (building curriculum architecture collaboratively) does not naturally transfer to the content ingestion phase — studying M01 material. This is a well-documented ADHD pattern; dopamine circuits respond to novelty and visible production, not accumulated understanding.

Four structural tactics identified for engineering dopamine into the study workflow:
1. **Production tasks during study** (write a proof, generate a summary, draw a diagram) — output visibility as dopamine anchor
2. **The H&C anchor question** ("how does this concept appear in the platform I'm building?") — purpose-activation interrupt that connects study to visible production work
3. **Live session logging** as parallel output stream — maintains "building something" feeling during content ingestion
4. **Low-friction entry points** (one worked example as the starting unit, not "study linear algebra") — bypasses initiation resistance by naming the smallest possible action

Key structural insight: session logging during study produces research data. The data IS the deliverable. This reframe converts studying from consumption to production — the same reframe that made this design session feel generative rather than effortful.

**RQ5 relevance:** Engagement architecture for neurodiverse learners must engineer output visibility into the study workflow by design, not treat it as a side effect of interest. This is a platform design requirement.

### [~10:42 EDT] Feedback Calibration Protocol — Deployed to Profile and Hermes SOUL

**Type:** behavioral-design · system-configuration
**RQ:** RQ5
**Tags:** feedback-calibration · correction-sensitivity · self-awareness · recognition-vs-edification · cognitive-vs-emotional-scaffolding

Subject articulated a clear preference against therapy-style support and external emotional hand-holding, self-identifying as preferring to adapt and learn independently rather than rely on external emotional scaffolding. This is an important nuance for the research frame: the need is for *cognitive* scaffolding (task initiation, external memory, verification), not *emotional* scaffolding (validation, reassurance, counseling). The two are structurally distinct.

Subject explicitly named a persistent internal conflict: work done for personal edification vs. work done for external recognition. The latter drive can dominate the internal narrative in ways that generate discouragement when recognition doesn't arrive. This was named as a self-aware dynamic, not a request for intervention. The subject distinguished between wanting recognition (legitimate) and treating recognition as a validity criterion for the work (distortion). Recognition is the amplifier; internal evidence is the proof.

Four feedback calibration principles confirmed by the subject and deployed to both AI systems:
1. Separate observation from verdict
2. Build-on-able framing
3. Don't amplify spiraling
4. Deliver honest assessments when asked

Changes committed to `profile/about.md` (Claude Code) and `tools/hermes/SOUL-template.md` (Hermes/Virgil). This is a structural system configuration, not a one-session instruction.

**RQ5 relevance:** Distinguishing cognitive scaffolding from emotional scaffolding is central to the extended mind thesis framing. RLHF sycophancy risk is highest when the system crosses the boundary into emotional scaffolding — this calibration explicitly defines and maintains that boundary. Data point for the risk architecture section of THESIS.md.
