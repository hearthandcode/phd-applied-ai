# Thesis Refocus Proposal

**Status: PROPOSAL — nothing in `THESIS.md` is changed by this document.** This file
argues for a refocus and specifies exactly what the new thesis statement would be. Applying
it to `THESIS.md` is a separate, human-approved step logged in `docs/THESIS-CHANGELOG.md`.

---

## 1. The one-sentence change

**FROM** a thesis about *designing an adaptive AI-native educational platform* (with a
neurodiverse learner as one supporting research question),
**TO** a thesis about *AI as a literal implementation and interpretation of the Extended
Mind thesis (Clark & Chalmers, 1998) for neurodivergent adults* — with the platform
demoted from the object of study to the **applied artifact** that the study generates.

The autoethnographic N=1 record (previously RQ5, a sub-question) becomes the **spine** of
the entire thesis, not a chapter within it.

## 3. Theoretical framing (the new core)

The refocused thesis sits on three interlocking literatures. The curriculum proposal adds
tracks to master each (see `curriculum-structure-proposal.md`).

### 3.1 Extended Mind & the constitution question
- **Clark & Chalmers (1998), *The Extended Mind*** — parity principle; the Otto/Inga
  notebook; the "trust and glue" conditions (reliably available, automatically endorsed,
  easily accessible, consciously endorsed in the past). The thesis asks whether a
  calibrated AI partner meets these conditions *better* than Otto's notebook, because it is
  active, bidirectional, and adaptive.
- **Clark (2003, 2008), *Natural-Born Cyborgs* / *Supersizing the Mind*** — humans as
  constitutively tool-coupled; scaffolding as the norm, not the exception.
- **The coupling–constitution objection (Adams & Aizawa, 2008; Rupert, 2004)** — the
  strongest opposing case: coupling to a resource is not the same as that resource being
  *part of* the mind. The thesis must engage this directly; the autoethnographic record is
  the evidence brought to bear (phenomenology of authorship, the felt boundary of "my
  thinking"). This is what makes nRQ1 a real question rather than an assertion.
- **Predictive processing / active externalism** as a bridge to the ML substrate (why an
  LLM is a plausible cognitive coupler, not just a database).

### 3.2 Cognitive scaffolding & neurodivergent cognition
- **Vygotsky — zone of proximal development / scaffolding**: the pedagogical ancestor of
  the extended-mind claim; scaffolding that is *internalized* vs. scaffolding that remains
  *external-but-constitutive* (the neurodivergent case may be permanently the latter, and
  that is not a deficit).
- **Executive function models (Barkley on ADHD as an executive-function / self-regulation
  condition; Brown's model)** — grounds *which* functions are being externalized: task
  initiation, working memory, temporal organization, affect regulation.
- **The social/neurodiversity model of disability** vs. the medical/deficit model — the
  thesis adopts the former: the disabling gap is between an atypical cognitive profile and
  an environment built for typical profiles; cognitive scaffolding re-shapes the
  environment rather than "fixing" the person.
- **Cognition-as-identity** — for this subject, the way he thinks *is* who he is; therefore
  extending cognition raises identity and agency questions (nRQ4) that a purely
  performance-framed study would miss.

### 3.3 Human augmentation, HCI, and the ethics of the prosthesis
- **Engelbart (1962), *Augmenting Human Intellect*** — the direct HCI lineage of
  "augmentation," now with a working substrate.
- **Distributed cognition (Hutchins), cognitive artifacts (Norman)** — the design-science
  vocabulary that lets N=1 phenomenology become platform design principles.
- **Ethics of cognitive extension** — dependence, deskilling, epistemic agency and
  offloading (Carr; recent work on AI reliance), autonomy for disabled users, and the
  transhumanist framing of assistive technology as enhancement. This grounds nRQ4 and the
  AI-ethics track.

## 4. Why the new framing is stronger and more focused

1. **It has an argument, not just a system.** "Does AI constitute an extended mind for this
   subject?" is a question with a real opposing side (Adams & Aizawa). "We built an
   adaptive platform" is not falsifiable and reads as product marketing. Committees and
   referees engage with the former.
2. **It matches the actual method.** The project *is* an N=1 autoethnography — that was
   already RQ5's method. Making it the spine removes the mismatch between a systems-flavored
   thesis title and a first-person qualitative dataset. The conflict-of-interest and
   research-drives-product commitments in the current `THESIS.md` become *cleaner*, because
   the platform is now explicitly downstream of the findings, not the object being defended.
3. **It resolves the generalization tension.** The old RQ1/RQ3/RQ4 quietly reached toward
   "for similar learner populations" and "design principles" — claims an N=1 cannot support.
   The new framing scopes everything to generative hypotheses and moves generalization into
   named future work, honestly.
4. **It is more original and more publishable in more venues.** A rigorous first-person
   account of AI as a *constitutive* cognitive scaffold for a neurodivergent adult is thin
   in the literature and spans philosophy of mind, disability studies, HCI, and AIED — more
   surface area for contribution than "another adaptive tutor."
5. **It keeps the Applied-AI/ML PhD intact.** The subject must still *master* the CS/AI
   stack — that mastery is the very phenomenon under study (a neurodivergent adult reaching
   doctoral competency via the scaffold). The technical curriculum is not decoration; it is
   the domain in which the extended-mind claim is tested. See curriculum proposal.
