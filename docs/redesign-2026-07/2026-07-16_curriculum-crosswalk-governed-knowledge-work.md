# Curriculum Crosswalk Proposal: Applied AI for Governed Knowledge Work

## Recognition

- **Why this exists:** The existing 67-module curriculum retains a strong technical spine, but its specialized phase remains organized around an education-platform thesis. This crosswalk preserves that spine while turning knowledge work, synthesis, organization, governance, provenance, and cognitive-workbench design into explicit research preparation.
- **Where it belongs:** `docs/redesign-2026-07/` as a proposal. The canonical sequencing and module inventory remain in `curriculum/overview.md`, `curriculum/phase-map.md`, `SCORECARD.md`, and the locked `docs/spec.md`.
- **What Scott should recognize:** Learning does not disappear. It becomes one demanding, meaningful form of knowledge work and one application context for the workbench, alongside research, engineering, creative work, and academic synthesis.
- **Papertrail:** Derived from the existing M01–M67 curriculum registry, the July Extended Mind curriculum proposal, and the new governed knowledge-workbench direction and source docket.
- **Verification state:** Draft. New module labels and maps are candidate design data only.

## Design rules

1. **Preserve M01–M67.** Existing IDs, audit paths, scorecard rows, prerequisites, and session references are durable historical infrastructure.
2. **Keep phases for sequencing.** Phases describe prerequisite and learning order. Tracks are cross-cutting metadata that describe why a module matters to the research program.
3. **Prefer data over duplicated prose.** Before changing documentation, define a versioned module and research-program data source from which maps, dashboards, prompts, and future generated prose can be derived.
4. **Do not generate curriculum content before the research questions and source-intake expectations are approved.** A module scaffold is not a harmless decoration if it commits terminology, scope, assessment, and reading claims.
5. **Keep learning alive without centering pedagogy.** AIED and learning-science modules continue as application and accessibility domains, not as the thesis's principal research object.
6. **Treat governance as technical and experiential.** A policy is only useful when its authority, enforcement point, review state, recovery path, and human burden are inspectable.

## Specialist-phase reframe

The current Phase 4 is not deleted. Its center of gravity changes from "AI in Education" to **Human, Governed, and Accessible AI-Supported Knowledge Work**.

| Existing cluster | Proposed role | Disposition |
|---|---|---|
| M43–M45: AIED, tutoring, adaptive learning | Application domain for knowledge work, learner agency, and adaptive support. | Retain and reframe. |
| M46–M49: curriculum, instructional design, AI literacy, AI pedagogy | Methods for learning as one form of knowledge work and for helping humans critically use AI. | Retain and reframe. |
| M50: gamification | Optional or elective design pattern, not a central causal theory of motivation. | Demote, do not delete. |
| M51: educational measurement | Useful evaluation-method input, especially for evidence and assessment design. | Retain as adjacent. |
| M52–M53: affective computing and personality modeling | Potential configuration and interaction inputs, never a proxy for the person or a hidden inference engine. | Retain as bounded adjacent work. |
| M54: virtue and vice modeling | Historical concept and possible critique case, not a research-program core. | Demote, do not delete. |
| M55–M57: AI ethics, fairness, society | Governance, accountability, power, and social impact context for knowledge-work systems. | Retain and strengthen. |
| M58–M59: cognitive science and HCI | The core human-facing theory and interaction-design basis for a cognitive workbench. | Promote. |
| M60: MLOps, AI engineering, experiment design | Operational evaluation, monitoring, reproducibility, and artifact delivery. | Promote. |
| M61: neurodiversity and AI-mediated learning | Disability-informed and neurodivergent design lens, plus bounded longitudinal case context. | Promote, while avoiding population claims from N=1. |

## Proposed generation order

This is a preparation order, not a commitment to generate every module in sequence.

1. **M68 and M69** establish the vocabulary, metadata, provenance, governance, and authority substrate.
2. **M70** establishes evidence synthesis and sensemaking before automation claims outrun evidence practice.
3. **M71 and M72** apply AI, retrieval, knowledge engineering, and agents to the governed substrate.
4. **M73** tests the human and accessibility interaction boundary before the system becomes a tooling-first artifact.
5. **M74** defines an evaluation protocol before treating accumulated artifact activity as research findings.
