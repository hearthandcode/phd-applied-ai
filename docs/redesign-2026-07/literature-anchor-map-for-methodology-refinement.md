# Literature Anchor Map for Methodology Refinement

## Status

This map incorporates the delegated literature-scouting batch returned on 2026-07-05. It is a scouting artifact, not a final bibliography. Items marked **checked** have been directly inspected or verified by fresh lookup in this session. Items marked **candidate** are useful but still need a full citation audit before they should appear in the thesis.

The article mentioned by the external correspondent has still not been provided. This map does not analyze that article.

## How to use this map

Use these sources to build three integrated thesis strands:

1. Extended Mind, AI scaffold, coupling conditions, and coupling failures.
2. Monotropism, neurodivergent attention, executive function, embodied cognition, and identity.
3. Autoethnographic credibility, thick description, reflexivity, and tool-as-instrument methods.

The synthesis goal is not three siloed literature-review sections. The goal is to show how a neurodivergent subject, an AI scaffold, and a longitudinal autoethnographic method form one methodological object.

### Strand 1 synthesis note

The thesis should not jump from "AI helped" to "AI is part of my mind." The stronger argument is graded:

1. The scaffold sometimes functions as useful assistance.
2. In some moments it becomes reliable external scaffolding.
3. In stronger moments it may meet functional integration criteria.
4. In failure moments it becomes alienating, load-increasing, or misleading.

Those boundaries are the contribution.

### Strand 2 synthesis note

The safest claim is not "cognition is identity" as a universal statement. The safer, stronger claim is:

> In this longitudinal record, cognitive style is identity-relevant because attention, agency, authorship, affect, and participation are shaped by the same neurodivergent patterns that the scaffold is designed to support.

The generalization pathway should be:

1. N of 1 thick description.
2. Candidate construct formation.
3. Design hypotheses for high-monotropism or AuDHD knowledge workers.
4. Future cohort study with explicit heterogeneity.

### Strand 3 synthesis note

The reflexivity problem should be explicit:

> The AI scaffold is a dual-role artifact. In instrument mode, it helps capture prompts, responses, timestamps, context recovery, and method changes. In phenomenon mode, it is the experienced cognitive scaffold being studied. The method must preserve both layers without collapsing one into the other.

This justifies the revised session-log structure:

1. Cognitive baseline.
2. Trigger and context.
3. Subject report.
4. AI action and tool state.
5. Affective and executive-function delta.
6. Coupling condition status.
7. Failure or friction tags.
8. Human verification.

## Methodological implications added by the delegated scouts

1. Add a **pre-session cognitive baseline** to high-signal captures when feasible.
2. Add a **post-session cognitive delta** after major scaffolding events.
3. Separate **flow** from **inertia**. Deep focus is not always good and stuckness is not always failure.
4. Treat **coupling failure** as a first-class data type: hallucination, context loss, latency, misalignment, alienation, and recovery action.
5. Use **human adjudication** for any AI-assisted theme generation.
6. Keep a **traceability matrix** from raw subject report to coded theme to thesis claim.
7. Avoid prosthesis language when it implies deficit correction. Prefer scaffold, affordance, niche, or containment architecture when the mechanism is environmental support.

4. What is the minimal traceability matrix that does not create logging fatigue?
5. Which coupling failures are already visible in the historical record?
6. Should future cohort work stratify by diagnosis, Monotropism Questionnaire score, or self-described attention style?
