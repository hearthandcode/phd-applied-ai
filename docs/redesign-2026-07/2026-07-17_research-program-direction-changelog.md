# Research Program Direction Changelog

## Status and scope

**Proposal.** This record describes a forward research-program direction. It does not edit or supersede `THESIS.md`, `METHODOLOGY.md`, historical session records, existing modules, scorecards, audit rows, or the locked curriculum specification.

## Directional change

| Earlier emphasis | Proposed emphasis | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| An adaptive AI-native education platform as the principal object of study. | Governed AI-augmented knowledge work and a provenance-aware cognitive workbench. | Learning remains a demanding application domain, but knowledge capture, synthesis, review, recovery, and human authority are now direct design concerns. |
| Platform effectiveness and learner adaptation at scale. | Inspectable transformations, source authority, provenance, review, correction, and recovery. | These mechanisms can be designed and evaluated without claiming population efficacy from a single case. |
| One autoethnographic strand carrying the full program. | Hybrid design science, research through design, longitudinal single-case work, autoethnography, and trace-based artifact analysis. | Each source of evidence can support a narrower, more defensible claim. |
| A particular agent runtime as the implied center of the artifact story. | An implementation-independent cognitive-workbench artifact, with agent runtimes treated as optional adapters. | The program needs portable records, governance, and recovery boundaries. |

## Proposed four-question architecture

1. **Knowledge-work breakdowns:** What recurring problems arise in AI-assisted capture, organization, retrieval, comparison, and synthesis of heterogeneous evidence?
2. **Governance and provenance mechanisms:** Which artifact-level mechanisms make sources, transformations, responsible agents, review states, uncertainty, and recovery paths inspectable without prohibitive correction load?
3. **Cognitive-workbench use:** In a longitudinal single-case record, how do those mechanisms affect context recovery, task startability, cognitive load, agency, and authorship? Which failure modes recur?
4. **Design principles and evaluation:** What bounded design principles and evaluation criteria follow from iterative artifact design, traceable use, and documented failures?

These are research questions, not settled findings. They do not assert that an AI is literally part of a mind or that an intervention works for a population.

## Curriculum implications

The proposed curriculum preserves M01-M67 and adds candidate modules M68-M83. The additions make knowledge organization, provenance, governance, evidence synthesis, agentic engineering, cognitive-workbench design, and human agency explicit program material. See the accompanying [83-module architecture](2026-07-17_curriculum-architecture-v2-83-modules.md).

Executive function/accessibility and motivation/self-regulation remain named cross-cutting lenses rather than becoming standalone modules. This limits curriculum inflation while keeping the concerns visible in the relevant technical, HCI, cognitive-science, research-method, and interaction-design modules.

## Method and evidence transition

| Evidence strand | Supports | Does not support alone |
|---|---|---|
| Design science research | Problem framing, artifact objectives, design, demonstration, and evaluation structure. | Population outcomes or broad adoption claims. |
| Research through design | Alternatives, tensions, and design exemplars. | Representative usability or efficacy claims. |
| Longitudinal single-case autoethnography | Subject-reported agency, authorship, cognitive load, continuity, and failure. | Population claims or a complete technical architecture. |
| Trace-based artifact analysis | Source, transformation, review, correction, and recovery-path coverage. | Subjective experience without explicit report. |
| Future consent-governed external evaluation | Variation across contexts and people. | Replacement for the originating first-person record. |

## Source limits

The curriculum orientation uses W3C PROV and SKOS, FAIR principles, NIST AI RMF, agent-system surveys, agent evaluation research, accessibility guidance, and philosophy reference sources. These sources help define scope and boundaries. They do not certify the proposed workbench, establish efficacy, or settle philosophical questions about cognition or agency.
