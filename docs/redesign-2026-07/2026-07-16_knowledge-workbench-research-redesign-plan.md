# Research Redesign Plan: Governed AI-Augmented Knowledge Workbench

## Recognition

- **Why this exists:** The existing July redesign correctly moved the thesis away from an education-platform-first claim and toward AI-mediated cognition. This plan takes the next evidence-preserving step: it treats knowledge synthesis, organization, governance, provenance, and returnable knowledge work as the primary research domain.

- **What Scott should recognize:** Learning and neurodivergent cognitive scaffolding remain essential. They become a design lens and a major evaluation context within a broader knowledge-work research program, rather than carrying the entire thesis scope alone.
- **Papertrail:** Built from the current `THESIS.md`, July Extended Mind proposal packet, curriculum maps, session-log protocol, and the Hub's T37/T38 governance and cognitive-workbench direction.
- **Verification state:** Draft. No canonical thesis text, locked curriculum spec, module, research-question source, or verified session record is changed by this plan.

## Objective

Reorient the doctoral program around the design and evaluation of a **governed, provenance-aware, AI-augmented cognitive workbench** for knowledge work.

The workbench should help a person capture, organize, retrieve, compare, synthesize, critique, and transform knowledge while preserving source authority, provenance, review status, authorship, uncertainty, and a path back to the originating evidence. Exocore is the prospective applied artifact. Hermes is a valuable but optional execution adapter, never the workbench's architectural owner.

The research should remain Applied AI/ML and agentic engineering in substance. Cognitive science, HCI, and neurodivergent experience supply theory, constraints, and evaluation context. Learning remains a living application domain because learning is sustained knowledge work, but it is no longer the entire subject matter.

## Working north-star statement

> How can a local-first, human-governed AI workbench support durable knowledge synthesis and organization without obscuring evidence, displacing authorship, or making provenance and recovery secondary concerns?

This is deliberately a research direction, not yet a thesis claim. It needs a source-checked literature and methods packet before it becomes canonical prose.

## Recommended thesis posture

### Primary contribution

A design and evaluation framework for AI-supported knowledge work in which **governance and provenance are first-class interaction primitives** rather than administrative afterthoughts.

### Applied artifact

A local-first Exocore cognitive workbench that can make the following inspectable:

- a claim's sources and transformations;
- the authority, review, and verification state of an artifact;
- which human or tool produced each transformation;
- what remains uncertain, contested, or pending review;
- how a person can recover a working thread after interruption;
- how automation is constrained by human approval and owned boundaries.

### Human and neurodivergent contribution

Neurodivergent knowledge work is not a narrow user-study add-on. It supplies non-negotiable design tests: does the workbench reduce cognitive load without reducing agency, make interruption recovery possible, preserve authorial ownership, and keep complex work resumable without surveillance or coercion?

### Extended Mind contribution

The existing Extended Mind frame remains useful as a theoretical lens for a bounded question: when does an AI-supported knowledge environment function as reliable scaffolding, and when does it become unreliable, alienating, or load-increasing? The research need not assert in advance that a generative system is literally a constituent of the mind.

## Candidate research question architecture for review

The current nRQ1–nRQ5 packet is valuable history, but is still centered on the constitution question and learning configuration. For this new direction, a four-question set is likely more cohesive and more manageable.

1. **Knowledge-work practice:** What recurring breakdowns occur when a researcher or knowledge worker captures, organizes, retrieves, compares, and synthesizes heterogeneous evidence with AI assistance?
2. **Governance and provenance:** Which provenance, authority, review, uncertainty, and recovery mechanisms make AI-mediated knowledge work more inspectable and governable without adding prohibitive correction or documentation load?
3. **Human-AI cognitive scaffolding:** In a longitudinal single-case record, how do those mechanisms affect context recovery, task startability, cognitive load, agency, and authorship for the researcher? What failure modes appear?
4. **Design and evaluation:** What reusable design principles and evaluation criteria for a local-first cognitive workbench follow from the artifact's iterative design, traceable use, and observed failures?

This shape preserves the N=1 record as evidence for Question 3 and as a source of design hypotheses. It stops making N=1 autoethnography the only method asked to justify systems, knowledge-governance, and technical design claims.

## Methodological direction to test against literature

The provisional recommendation is a **hybrid program**, not a choice between autoethnography and rigor:

1. **Design science / research-through-design:** design, build, and document knowledge-workbench artifacts and their intended mechanisms.
2. **Longitudinal single-case study:** use one bounded research environment, with explicit task classes and repeated observation.
3. **Reflexive autoethnography:** preserve Scott's first-person experience of agency, cognitive load, continuity, and authorship where only the subject can report it.
4. **Trace-based artifact analysis:** inspect source paths, transformations, review states, recovery paths, and correction work without equating telemetry with experience.
5. **Future external evaluation:** reserve cohort, comparative, or collaborator studies for a later consent-governed phase. Do not promise population-level findings from the current corpus.

The method packet must state what each strand can support. A personal record can support thick description and design hypotheses. It cannot establish general efficacy for neurodivergent users. An artifact trace can demonstrate provenance coverage and recovery behavior. It cannot prove subjective agency without the participant's report.

## Decision gates

1. **Research center:** Does knowledge work and governed synthesis become the primary domain, with learning retained as an application thread?
2. **Method:** Is the hybrid design-science plus longitudinal-single-case approach acceptable, with autoethnography retained but not made solely responsible for the thesis?
3. **Thesis claim:** Should the thesis investigate conditions of cognitive scaffolding rather than pre-assert a strong Extended Mind constitution conclusion?
4. **Artifact boundary:** Is Exocore the named applied artifact, independent of Hermes, with the Hub's Markdown/YAML record patterns as field material?
5. **Curriculum:** Should the existing M-series remain stable while cross-cutting tracks and eventual additions are modeled as data?
6. **Governance:** Which data is public, private, review-gated, or never captured? This must be explicit before adding telemetry or external evaluation.
