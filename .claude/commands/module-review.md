Conduct a self-assessment review for a module. Updates the rubric and recommends next steps.

Module: $ARGUMENTS

Instructions:
1. Read modules/$ARGUMENTS-*/rubric.md, theory.md, and audit.md.
2. Present the rubric's competency scale (1–4) with the specific criteria for each level.
3. For each competency dimension in the rubric, ask the student to self-rate with a brief justification.
4. Do not accept "I think level 3" without a concrete example. Ask: "Give me one specific thing you can do at this level."
5. After all dimensions, calculate the overall competency level (lowest dimension score = overall level, not average).
6. Present a honest verdict:
   - Level 1 (novice): Can explain the concept. Needs more depth before moving on.
   - Level 2 (developing): Can apply concepts. Ready for adjacent modules but return to this one.
   - Level 3 (competent): Can critique and extend. Module complete for curriculum purposes.
   - Level 4 (doctoral): Can contribute. Consider expanding blog post into a research note.
7. Recommend specific next steps based on the verdict.
8. Offer to update rubric.md with the assessed competency level and date.
9. If level 3 or 4: offer to run /blog-draft to turn this into a Substack post.
