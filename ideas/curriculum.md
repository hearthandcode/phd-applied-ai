# The Seven Fires of Mastery
## A Hearth & Code Curriculum Framework

> **For the self-directed learner who wants the depth of a doctorate without the rigidity of a traditional institution — designed for your cognitive profile, paced to your energy, built around your life.**

---

## The concept

Traditional doctoral programs are built around a single assumption: that all minds learn the same way at the same pace. The curriculum is fixed. The timeline is fixed. The assessment is one-size-fits-all.

Hearth & Code's framework inverts that assumption. The curriculum is adaptive. The pace is yours. The assessment measures genuine understanding, not seat time. The Seven Fires of Mastery are not levels you climb once and leave behind — they're states you move through, revisit, and deepen as your understanding grows.

The image is a hearth. Each fire stage represents a different relationship to knowledge — from the first spark of curiosity to the point where you're the one keeping the fire burning for someone else.

---

## The Seven Fires

### Fire 1 — The Ember

**The spark of intention.** You know you want more — more depth, more rigor, more mastery — but you don't yet know what that looks like or what it requires. The Ember is orientation: understanding the landscape, choosing your path, committing to the work.

**What happens here:**
- Self-assessment — where are you now, where do you want to go
- Cognitive profile intake — how does your mind actually learn
- Curriculum orientation — what's available, what's required, what's optional
- First conversation with your advisor (the committee system)

**ADHD adaptation:** The Ember is high-curiosity, low-execution-demand. This is where the dopamine of "new thing" is strongest — and where we capture it as fuel rather than letting it dissipate. One intake, one profile, one commitment.

**Brand voice:** "You walked through the door. That's the hardest step. We'll figure out the rest together."

---

### Fire 2 — The Kindling

**Building the foundation.** Every structure needs a base. For AI and ML, that base is mathematical maturity and computational thinking — linear algebra, calculus, probability, CS fundamentals. This is the slowest fire because every concept here is load-bearing for everything above it.

**What happens here:**
- Phase 0: Mathematical Foundations (M01-M06)
- Phase 1: CS Fundamentals (M07-M18)
- Symbolic notation as a learned language, not a native one

**ADHD adaptation:** Kindling is where most self-directed programs fail. The material is dry, the payoff is distant, and the ADHD brain craves novelty. The H&C adaptation: every concept in Kindling is immediately connected to something in a higher fire. You learn field axioms, and the same session shows you how they constrain the scoring system in the platform. The abstraction has a home.

**Brand voice:** "This is the least glamorous fire. It's also the most important. Every concept here is a foundation stone — if it's wobbly, everything above it wobbles. We don't rush this fire. We don't skip it. But we also don't make it miserable."

---

### Fire 3 — The Flame

**Active mastery.** Machine learning. Neural networks. Attention. Transformers. The material that brought you here. This is the consuming fire — papers, implementations, experiments, and the joy of things clicking into place.

**What happens here:**
- Phase 2: ML Foundations (M19-M28)
- Phase 3: Advanced AI (M29-M42)
- Every module has a computational project tied to platform design

**ADHD adaptation:** This fire rewards the ADHD superpower — pattern-matching, divergent thinking, cross-domain connection. The challenge is depth over breadth. The flame wants to consume everything — the framework channels that hunger into structured depth.

**Brand voice:** "This is the fire you came for. The concepts are beautiful, the math is elegant, and the implementations are real. But the Flame is also where most people burn out — they consume without digesting. We pace it. Not to slow you down, but to make sure it sticks."

---

### Fire 4 — The Blaze

**Choosing a thread.** General knowledge narrows into specialized research. You pick a direction — AI in education, cognitive modeling, adaptive systems, whatever calls you — and you go deep. Original thinking begins here.

**What happens here:**
- Phase 4: Specialized Research (M43-M61)
- First pass at defining your research question
- Identifying gaps in existing work

**ADHD adaptation:** The Blaze is dangerous for the ADHD mind. The field opens up and offers a thousand threads. The H&C framework doesn't force a single choice — it lets you explore up to three threads in parallel before converging. The system tracks what energizes you and what drains you, and helps you choose accordingly.

**Brand voice:** "This is where you stop being a student of the field and start becoming a contributor. It's also where the path forks a thousand ways. We don't pick for you — but we do help you see which paths have been walked, which are overgrown, and which are yours to blaze."

---

### Fire 5 — The Forge

**Shaping knowledge into contribution.** Research methodology. Systematic review. Original experiments. Architecture decisions. This is where you build something that didn't exist before.

**What happens here:**
- Research methodology (M62)
- Systematic literature review (M63)
- Thesis proposal (M64)
- The shift from consuming to producing

**ADHD adaptation:** The Forge requires sustained attention over months — the hardest thing for the ADHD brain. The framework breaks this into checkpoints: a literature review isn't "read 200 papers" — it's "find 10 papers on this subtopic, synthesize, move to the next subtopic." Each checkpoint produces an artifact. Each artifact is a visible win.

**Brand voice:** "The Forge is the fire that doesn't go out until you're done. It's sustained, it's hard, and it's the most rewarding fire of the seven. The secret is that we don't expect you to stay in the Forge alone — the Circle is here. The Hearth is always on."

---

### Fire 6 — The Anvil

**The thesis and defense.** Everything you've learned, everything you've built, everything you've discovered — brought together into one document, presented to your committee, defended under scrutiny.

**What happens here:**
- Thesis writing (M65-M66)
- Defense preparation (M67)
- The document that proves you can do this alone

**ADHD adaptation:** The Anvil is the greatest executive function challenge. The thesis is a multi-month writing project with no external deadlines. The H&C framework provides: writing sprints (not endless sessions), per-chapter checkpoints, an AI writing partner that helps you structure, and a committee simulation that gives you practice defending before the real thing.

**Brand voice:** "The Anvil is where everything gets shaped. Not created — shaped. All the raw material from the earlier fires is here. Your job is to strike it, reshape it, strike again, until it's true. The Hearth doesn't swing the hammer for you — but it holds the metal steady."

---

### Fire 7 — The Torch

**Passing it forward.** Mastery isn't complete until you've guided someone else through the same journey. The Torch is mentorship, teaching, community contribution. You become the one who keeps the hearth burning for the next traveler.

**What happens here:**
- Mentoring new learners in the Circle (community)
- Publishing research findings
- Contributing back to the platform — curriculum review, feature feedback, pedagogical design
- Becoming a Firetender

**ADHD adaptation:** The Torch taps into the ADHD motivation that traditional education neglects — purpose. Teaching cements understanding. Mentoring creates accountability. Community provides the external structure that executive function struggles to generate internally.

**Brand voice:** "You made it. Now there are others at the threshold, and they're looking for the fire. You don't have to be a perfect master — you just have to be willing to hold the torch long enough for them to light their own. The Circle grows."

## How it works in practice

### Mastery Spiral, not Mastery Ladder

The Seven Fires are not a ladder you climb once. They're a spiral. You'll reach the Blaze (Fire 4) and discover that your Kindling (Fire 2) needs reinforcement. You'll start the Forge (Fire 5) and realize your Flame (Fire 3) has gaps. That's not failure — that's the design. Every level informs every other level. The framework expects re-entry.

### Cognitive profile routing

The framework starts with a cognitive profile (Fire 1: The Ember) that determines:
- **Formalism density** — how much notation vs. intuition in the sessions
- **Exercise modality** — computational (Python/NumPy) over paper-only
- **Session pacing** — checkpoint size, sprint length, energy calibration
- **Interest domains** — which modules get domain-flavored examples

Your profile is not static. It updates as you work through the fires. A learner who started at formalism density 0.3 in The Kindling might naturally increase to 0.6 by The Flame.

### The role of AI

AI plays different roles at different fires:

| Fire | AI role | Interaction mode |
|---|---|---|
| The Ember | Orientation guide | Socratic intake interview |
| The Kindling | Patient tutor | Explanation on demand, check-in pacing |
| The Flame | Research partner | Literature search, concept synthesis, code generation |
| The Blaze | Hypothesis sparring | Adversarial challenge, gap analysis |
| The Forge | Methodology advisor | Experiment design, reproducibility checks |
| The Anvil | Writing collaborator | Structure, draft, revision, defense practice |
| The Torch | Community scaffold | Mentoring tools, peer matching, contribution tracking |

### How this maps to the curriculum

## Marketing positioning

This framework differentiates H&C from every other doctoral-adjacent program in three ways:

1. **Cognitive profile-first.** No other program asks "how does your mind learn?" before designing your curriculum. This is the core differentiator.

2. **ADHD-aware by design, not accommodation.** Most programs add accommodations after the fact. H&C is built from the ground up for the executive function profile that traditional education excludes.

3. **The Circle as graduation requirement.** You don't finish at The Anvil. You finish when you've passed the torch. This transforms the endpoint from "I got my degree" to "I helped someone else get theirs."

**Tagline for the framework:** *Seven fires. One pace — yours.*
