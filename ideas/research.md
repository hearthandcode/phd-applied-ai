### [2026-06-14 ~20:00] COI and research-product entanglement — the feedback loop problem

**Domain:** research
**Source:** Side conversation during M01 learning session
**Trigger:** Question about legal, ethical, and methodological concerns of tying research, curriculum, and product together

The self-fulfilling loop risk: researcher adjusts curriculum when something is hard → it becomes easier → researcher concludes design worked. But the researcher did the adjusting. Mitigation logged: treat the design evolution itself as data, not confound. The CHANGELOG and session logs are evidence of the adjustment process, not evidence of validity.

Deeper question for M62: is there a way to distinguish between "this design worked" and "this learner adapted to this design"? Learning is always partly accommodation. The question is whether the design change produced genuine comprehension or just reduced friction without improving understanding. May need a competency-verification protocol that is independent of the curriculum delivery format.

**Follow-up needed:** yes — design a competency verification method for M62 that doesn't rely on self-report alone (oral exam simulation with the committee is the existing mechanism — note this explicitly)
**Related:** RQ5, M62 (Research Methodology), committee oral exam design

### [2026-06-14 ~20:00] Research-drives-product principle — what "following the evidence" actually means in practice

**Domain:** research
**Source:** THESIS.md methodology section drafting
**Trigger:** "I want the research to drive the product, not the product drive the research"

The principle is stated. The harder question is the operationalization: what does "following the evidence" look like when the evidence comes from N=1 and the product will serve N=many? The path from "this didn't work for me" to "this shouldn't be in the platform" is not direct.

Proposed heuristic: autoethnographic evidence warrants platform changes when:
(a) the failure mode is mechanistic (e.g. format-mismatch causes measurable comprehension drop) rather than preference-based (e.g. "I liked this style better"), AND
(b) the mechanism is plausibly generalizable to the target learner population based on existing literature

This heuristic should be stated explicitly in M62 and in the platform's design rationale documentation.

**Follow-up needed:** yes — formalize this heuristic before first platform design decision based on research findings
**Related:** M62, research-drives-product principle in THESIS.md, RQ1
